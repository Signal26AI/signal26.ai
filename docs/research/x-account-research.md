## Winning Patterns

### Content Types (ranked by effectiveness)
1. **Product demo videos/GIFs** — Before/after workflows. Highest engagement across all accounts.
2. **Revenue/metrics screenshots** — Creates FOMO and social proof.
3. **Developer memes** — Niche humor = tribal identification = follows.
4. **Build-in-public updates** — Makes followers invested in your journey.
5. **Code snippet images** — Carbon/Snappify. Speaks louder than text for devs.
6. **Hot takes / opinions** — Drives replies (algorithm loves replies).
7. **Community amplification** — RTing users creates reciprocal loyalty.
8. **Failure stories** — Vulnerability builds trust.

### Tone & Voice
- Casual over corporate — sound like a person, never a press release
- Technical-but-accessible — dev jargon signals tribe, don't be exclusionary
- Self-deprecating humor — indie devs bond over shared struggles
- Aspirational but honest — show wins AND losses
- Personality-forward — distinct voice > polished brand

### Posting Frequency
| Tier | Frequency | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| High volume | 5–15/day | Being terminally online (Pieter) |
| Moderate | 2–5/day | Sweet spot for indie founders |
| Quality-focused | 1–3/day | Brand accounts, less extroverted founders |
| Minimal | 3–5/week | Only if product demos are killer |

**Consensus:** 1–3 quality posts/day is the sustainable sweet spot for an indie dev tool brand.

### Engagement Drivers
1. Ask questions that let devs show off
2. Revenue transparency — nothing drives engagement like real numbers
3. Tease upcoming features with screenshots
4. Reply to every comment in first hour (algorithm boost)
5. Quote-tweet interesting takes (adds your perspective)

---

## What Flops
1. **Corporate voice** — "We're excited to announce..." = instant scroll-past
2. **Thread spam** — Forced "10 lessons I learned" threads without substance
3. **Engagement bait without value** — "Like if you agree" without insight
4. **Too much self-promotion** — >20% promotional = unfollows
5. **Inconsistency** — Posting 5x/day for a week then disappearing
6. **Ignoring replies** — One-way broadcasting kills community
7. **Generic dev tips** — "Always write tests" level content is noise
8. **Overproduced content** — Polished graphics feel corporate, raw feels authentic

## Recommendations for Signal26

### Profile Setup
- **Handle:** @Signal26AI or @Signal26_
- **Bio:** Focus on what you help with, not what you are. "Tools for indie devs who ship. ShipPolicy • ShipLint • ShipIcon"
- **Profile pic:** Logo mark, works at tiny sizes, memorable color
- **Banner:** Product screenshots or tagline, dark background
- **Pinned tweet:** Best product demo video or a "who we are" thread

### Content Pillars (4)
1. **Product updates & demos** (~25%) — What we shipped, GIFs, before/after
2. **iOS dev tips & pain points** (~30%) — App Store rejection stories, privacy manifest gotchas, things AI-generated code misses
3. **Build-in-public** (~25%) — Revenue updates, decisions, failures, what we're working on
4. **Developer humor** (~20%) — Memes about App Store rejections, Xcode pain, vibe coding fails

### Week 1 Playbook
- Day 1: "Who we are" intro tweet + pinned
- Day 2: First product demo (ShipPolicy flow, 30-sec video)
- Day 3: iOS dev tip (App Store rejection stat + how to avoid)
- Day 4: Meme (App Store review pain)
- Day 5: Build-in-public update (what we're shipping this week)
- Day 6-7: Engage in iOS dev conversations, quote-tweet, reply

### Growth Tactics
- Follow and engage with iOS dev accounts (RevenueCat, Sindre Sorhus, Paul Hudson, etc.)
- Reply to App Store rejection complaints with genuine help (not plugging product)
- Cross-promote from Reddit/HN launches
- Use the 80/20 rule: 80% value/entertainment, 20% product
